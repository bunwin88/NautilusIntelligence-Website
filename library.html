Review method catalog

Core methods behind the diligence workflow.

These are working review patterns, not standalone claims. Each method is designed to preserve the source, status, and next action.

Data room inventory

Identify files, entities, plan years, duplicates, missing items, and documents requiring manual review.

Document classification

Separate guides, SBCs, SPDs, wrap documents, invoices, rate sheets, census files, and responses.

Carrier and vendor map

Connect benefit lines to carriers, PBMs, administrators, funding arrangements, and effective periods.

Plan design comparison

Compare matched plans across networks, deductibles, out-of-pocket limits, copays, coinsurance, and Rx tiers.

Rate and contribution capture

Normalize composite, tiered, age-banded, and location-specific structures without losing source labels.

Invoice reconciliation

Compare billed amounts, enrollment, rates, and contribution schedules and preserve unexplained variance.

Compliance screening

Organize plan document, Form 5500, COBRA, Section 125, notice, and administration review items.

Evidence reference log

Link findings to files, pages, excerpts, confidence, reviewer status, and superseding evidence.

DRL management

Track request wording, priority, workstream, owner, due date, response, and final resolution.

Buyer and target comparison

Add buyer information to the same deal record and compare program design, cost, and integration choices.

Formal report support

Convert validated analysis into program overview, expenses, portability, compliance, and follow-up sections.

Post-close action tracking

Turn findings into owners, timing, dependencies, decisions, and 30/60/90-day implementation work.

Important:Public examples are illustrative and are not legal, tax, ERISA, accounting, actuarial, or transaction advice. A client review must be scoped to the actual documents, transaction structure, and professional team.
