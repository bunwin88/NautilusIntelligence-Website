Engagement process

Start small, define the evidence, and expand only where the review supports it.

Nautilus begins with scope and source reality—not a generic automation promise. The process identifies the decision to support, the evidence available, the review standard, and the output that will actually be used.

01

Free Fit Review

Share transaction context, timing, users, current workflow, document categories, and desired output. No employee-level or confidential files are needed for this step.

02

Scope and acceptance criteria

Define the review population, document set, transaction structure, questions to answer, known limitations, evidence requirements, and delivery format.

03

Controlled pilot

Run a bounded document information review, validate extraction and mapping, resolve exceptions, and confirm whether the output is decision-useful.

04

Managed diligence workflow

Add follow-up documents, buyer information, seller responses, DRL resolution, report development, and recurring status management inside the same deal structure.

05

Post-close conversion

Translate confirmed findings into implementation decisions, owner assignments, deadlines, plan marketing inputs, and 30/60/90-day actions.

Good fit indicators

Where the approach adds the most value.

  • Multiple document types must be reconciled
  • Review quality depends on source and page traceability
  • Buyer and target information arrives in phases
  • Open items need owners, status, and resolution history
  • The final output must support both diligence and integration
Prepare for the first conversation

Bring the process, not the confidential data.

Useful inputs include:

  • Transaction type and approximate employee count
  • Target and buyer roles
  • Available document categories and plan years
  • Current deadline and report audience
  • Known pain points or prior review gaps
  • Desired first output
