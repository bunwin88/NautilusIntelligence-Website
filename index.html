What Nautilus Diligence does

From data room disorder to a reviewable benefits picture.

Benefits diligence fails when documents are reviewed in isolation. Nautilus connects the plan, financial, compliance, transaction, and follow-up layers.

01

Inventory the evidence

Classify benefits guides, SBCs, SPDs, wrap documents, invoices, rate sheets, census files, seller responses, and call notes.

02

Reconcile plans and cost

Map carriers and vendors, compare plan designs, capture rates and contributions, and surface mismatches across sources.

03

Screen transaction risk

Evaluate portability, COBRA responsibility, missing plan documents, funding questions, compliance gaps, and unresolved assumptions.

04

Convert findings into action

Produce evidence-linked workpapers, a focused issue summary, a living DRL, and post-close ownership and timing.

Built for the real deal room

The answer is rarely in one document.

A plan may be described one way in an open enrollment guide, another way in an SBC, billed differently on an invoice, and clarified later in a seller response. Nautilus preserves those differences instead of flattening them into an unsupported summary.
